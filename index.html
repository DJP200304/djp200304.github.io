Welcome

Malware can be a scary prospect when discovering the term for the first time; but no need to worry, this website will tell you everything you possibly need to know about different types of malware and how you can potentially protect yourself (don't panic you are not in immediate danger, alot of our protection goes on without us knowing!)

We will Go Through:

  • Trojans
  • Viruses
  • Worms
  • Spyware
  • Adware
  • Ransomware
  • Phishing

Trojans Not a Horse.

Unlike viruses trojans cannot replicate themselves. Instead they are disguised as a harmless piece of software available to download, often for free. Then once installed it will have a similar impact to a virus. This can be avoided by ensuring downloads and email attachments are from legitimate and trusted sources, if unsure; research

Worms Slimy.

Similar to viruses and a subset of trojans but they are not hidden inside other programs and don't require human activation, often spread through emails or through your local network. Again, being aware of the sources of your data goes along way towards avoiding this piece of malware

Viruses The Imposters.

Viruses work very much like the viruses you will know from the natural world; they hide inside other files and replicate themselves to become part of other programs, once activated by hand. They can cause damage by deleting or modifying files. The best defence against viruses is, believe it or not, anti-virus software, which checks programs on your computer against any known viruses for similarities.

Adware The Annoying Sibling.

Adware causes pop-ups or windows that will not go away, hence making them the annoying sibling of the malware world. They make their way onto your computer much like many of the other malware.

Ransomware Pay Up.

ransomware hijacks the data of a system through encryption then demands a price for decryption. This is often targetted at larger companies so shouldn't be too much of a worry but again can be stopped through up to date anti-virus and a knowledge of phishing.

Spyware Always Watching.

Spyware does exactly what it says on the tin, watches your activity such as passwords entered, sites visited then feeds this information back to a perpetrator. This can be picked up by most anti-virus but there is specialist detection software also out there.

Phishing The Mastermind.

This is where an email or text is sent with the intention of seeming legitimate but contains a link or attachment which is malicious. They are often very easy to spot however due to their many tell-tale signs:

  • Spelling Mistakes
  • Poor Grammar
  • Never know any specific details such as your name
  • Incorrect email addresses
  • General lack of professionalism

So, the next time you receive a suspicious email, just think and check for the signs before clicking any links or sending any details.
